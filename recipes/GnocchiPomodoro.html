His Highness "lo Gnocco"

Recipe for Gnocchi al pomodoro

Description

Ah, the delightful dance of dough and potatoes, twirling together in a tantalizing tango of flavor – that's gnocchi for you! This beloved Italian dish has a history as rich as its taste.

Legend has it that gnocchi dates back to ancient Roman times when clever cooks repurposed leftover bread into little dumplings. But it wasn't until the introduction of the humble potato from the New World that gnocchi truly soared to starchy stardom.

Picture this: Italy, centuries ago, hungry peasants pondering what to do with their potatoes. Lo and behold, someone had a stroke of culinary genius – mix those spuds with flour, roll 'em into little pillows, and boil 'em up. Thus, gnocchi was born, a dish so comforting it could make even the grumpiest nonna crack a smile.

Now, why is gnocchi such a classic, you ask? Well, aside from being downright delicious, gnocchi embodies everything we love about Italian cuisine – simplicity, tradition, and a whole lot of amore. It's versatile too! You can dress it up with a rich tomato sauce, smother it in creamy Gorgonzola, or keep it classic with a simple sage and butter drizzle.

Ingredients

For the gnocchi

  • 1kg of potatoes
  • 100gr of parmesan
  • 6g of salt
  • 200g of flour
  • 1 egg

For the sauce

  • 500g tomato sauce
  • 6 leaves of basil
  • 1 onion
  • salt and parmesan as needed

How do I cook it?

  1. Prepare the dough
    • Start boiling the potatoes until tender, then peel and mash them.
    • Allow the mashed potatoes to cool slightly before adding flour, egg, and a pinch of salt
    • Knead the mixture gently untila soft, smooth dought forms.
  2. Shape the gnocchi
    • Divide the dough into manageable portions and roll each piece into a long, thin rope on a floured surface
    • Cut the ropes into bite-size pieces, then gently press each one with a fork to create the classic ridges that help hold sauce
      Cook the gnocchi
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to boil
    • Drop the gnocchi into the boiling water in batches, being carefull not to overcrowd the pot
    • Once the gnocchi are floating to the surface let them cook for an additional minute or two, then remove them with a holed spoon and on to a serving dish
  3. Prepare the sauce
    • In a separate saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat and sautè minced garlic until fragrant
    • Add crushed tomatoes (or tomato sauce), a pinch of sugar, and a handful of fresh basil leaves to the pan
    • Simmer the sauce for roughly 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly

  4. Serve and enjoy!
